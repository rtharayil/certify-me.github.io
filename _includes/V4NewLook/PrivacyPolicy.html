15. FERPA Data Protection Rights

CertifyMe is committed to maintaining the confidentiality and security of student educational records in compliance with the Family Educational Rights and Privacy Act (FERPA). For educational institutions and students using our platform, the following FERPA rights apply:

  • The Right to Access: Students or their parents (if the student is under 18) have the right to access and review the student’s educational records maintained by educational institutions.
  • The Right to Request Amendment: Students or their parents may request corrections to the student’s educational records if they believe the information is inaccurate, misleading, or violates privacy rights.
  • The Right to Consent to Disclosures: Personally identifiable information from student records will not be disclosed without consent, except in cases where FERPA authorizes disclosure without consent (e.g., disclosure to school officials with legitimate educational interests).
  • The Right to File a Complaint: If an educational institution using CertifyMe fails to comply with FERPA requirements, students or parents have the right to file a complaint with the U.S. Department of Education.

CertifyMe enables educational institutions to handle student records in a way that meets FERPA compliance requirements. For further information on FERPA compliance or to exercise these rights, please contact us at info@certifyme.online.
